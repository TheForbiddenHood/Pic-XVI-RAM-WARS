# HOW TO PLAY

## Heads Up Display
There are 3 icons on the left of different values on the player HUD at the top of the OLED screen in *yellow*. From left to right as follows; **Diamond**: this is your currency for the game - this icon is meant to represent Corundum, and it maxes out at **999**. **Sword**: this is how much your current army's strength is valued at. **Hourglass**: this is how much time before your next engagement with the enemy. Watching this is vital to ensuring your success in the game.

### Corundum
At the beginning of a game, you will gain *+1* Corundum a second, to increase this you need more MNRs.

### Army Strength
Army Strength is determined based on *((number of MAR)+(number of MDR))(ROID level)*

### Hourglass
The timer is always 120 seconds. This does not change across the amount of rounds, or differ between rounds.

## Context Menus
There are **three** context menus in the game currently, there is the Command Center, the Barracks, and the Engineering Bay, in that order. What each one does and how they work will be explained below.

### Command Center
At the Command Center, players can either *purchase* another MNR, or *sell* a MNR they currently have. Purchasing a MNR costs 20 Corundum, and adds *+1* Corundum collection a second. Selling a MNR will *decrease* your Corundum collection by *-1* a second and return you **half** the cost of the MNR.

### Barracks
At the Barracks, players can either purchase a MAR or a MDR. The MAR costs 40 Corundum, and the MDR costs 100. 
**MAR is worth 1 Strength**
**MDR is worth 3 Strength**

### Engineering Bay
As explained before, RIOD level is important to your Army Strength value, which can be upgraded here. At the Engineering Bay, players can either purchase a *10%* increase to their DRIL level *((MNR amount)(DRIL level))*, or increase their ROID level, *((number of MAR)+(number of MDR))(ROID level)*. Both consistently cost 50 Corundum, and does not increase per upgrade level.

## How Do Battles Work?
When the timer reaches zero, the battle commences. This means that whatever your current army strength value is will be tested against the alien threat. If your army value is lower than the alien threat, you will lose the battle. If it is higher, you will succeed and keep the excess troops who survive the bloodshed. Your upgrades **will** reset. So keep that in mind as a temporary buff for your troops heading into the warzone. Those who return are not the same as they were.

## How do you Win?
Win **2/3** of the battles and you win! If you win the first two you'll never see the third battle since you swept the alien threat, otherwise prepare to hunker down for a really difficult battle.
